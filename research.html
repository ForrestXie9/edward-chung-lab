UMI Lab

Research

Our work connects AI, simulation, traffic engineering, connected autonomous vehicles, and urban mobility systems. The themes below are not exhaustive; we welcome new ideas that fit the broader vision of urban mobility intelligence.

Directions

Research Directions

01

Urban traffic intelligence

Traffic prediction, OD estimation, EV charging demand prediction, detector reliability, uncertainty quantification, and network-level mobility analytics.

02

Connected and autonomous mobility

Cooperative freeway merging, CAV trajectory coordination, mixed traffic operations, and connected ITS under communication constraints.

03

AI optimisation and digital twins

Traffic signal control, EV charging queueing and scheduling optimisation, transport-energy coupled optimisation, simulation-based optimisation, surrogate modelling, LLM-assisted algorithm design, and digital twin platforms.

04

Public transport and human mobility

Transit signal priority, bus reliability, pedestrian movement modelling, Bluetooth sensing, and urban activity analysis.

Projects

Projects and Grants

Project

Traffic Signal Control Evaluation-Diagnosis-Optimisation Driven by AI and Digital Twin

Developing a digital twin platform for urban road traffic management with real-time analysis, predictive modelling, and closed-loop optimisation.

Project

LLM-assisted Algorithm Design for Simulation-based Optimisation

Using large language models to design efficient optimisation algorithms for computationally expensive transport simulation models.

Project

Deep Learning Surrogate-based Traffic Signal Optimisation

Replacing expensive simulation-based performance functions with deep learning surrogate models for faster signal optimisation.

Project

Transit Signal Priority Using Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

Designing cooperative transit signal priority strategies that balance bus reliability with general traffic performance.

Project

Cooperative On-Ramp Merging for Connected Autonomous Vehicles

Coordinating lane-changing decisions, vehicle sequences, and trajectories for CAVs and mixed traffic at freeway on-ramps.

Project

Network-level Traffic Prediction Considering Detector Reliability and Limited Training Data

Building robust traffic prediction methods using clustering, few-shot learning, transfer learning, and detector reliability modelling.
