About

Welcome to Edward's webpage

Prof Edward Chung is Professor of Intelligent Transport Systems at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University. He is an internationally recognised expert in the application of ITS, with many years of experience as an engineer, academic, and researcher working nationally and internationally.

Principal Investigator

Prof Edward Chung

B. Eng. (Hons), PhD, FIEAust

Prior to joining PolyU, Edward was the inaugural Director of the Smart Transport Research Centre and Professor of Intelligent Transport Systems at Queensland University of Technology. He holds a Bachelor of Civil Engineering with Honours and a PhD from Monash University.

Research

Research Areas

01

Origin-destination prediction using knowledge-driven deep learning

A hierarchical framework predicts outflow first, then estimates flow probabilities through a gravity-inspired network to obtain real-time OD matrices.

02

LLM-assisted algorithm design for simulation-based optimisation

Large language models are used to design computationally efficient optimisers for high-fidelity transportation simulation and optimisation.

03

Traffic signal control evaluation, diagnosis, and optimisation

AI and digital twin technology support real-time analysis, predictive modelling, and closed-loop traffic management.

04

Deep learning surrogate-based traffic signal optimisation

Deep learning surrogate functions approximate simulation-based performance functions and improve optimisation efficiency.

05

Transit signal priority with multi-agent reinforcement learning

Cooperative TSP strategies adjust phase sequence and duration while balancing bus and car operations under real-time conditions.

06

Network-level traffic prediction under detector uncertainty

LSTM, multilayer perceptron, clustering, few-shot learning, and transfer learning are combined for prediction with unreliable and limited data.

07

Cooperative on-ramp merging for connected autonomous vehicles

Mixed integer nonlinear programming and decomposition algorithms coordinate lane changes, vehicle sequences, and trajectories.

08

Uncertainty quantification for traffic-related prediction

Deep learning models provide confidence intervals for bus arrival times, OD patterns, and road-segment traffic flows.

09

Modelling people's movements with Bluetooth sensor technology

Bluetooth scanning is used to model pedestrian mobility patterns and understand how people navigate urban environments.
